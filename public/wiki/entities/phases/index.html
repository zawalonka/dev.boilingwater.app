Phases

What phases are and how they behave in the simulation

View orphan phases report

Phases of matter describe how particles are arranged and how they move. In the game, phase-specific properties (density, heat capacity, latent heat, vapor pressure) control how each substance heats, cools, evaporates, and boils.

Educational notes

Understanding phases helps connect microscopic particle motion to macroscopic behavior. In this sandbox, each phase changes how energy moves (conduction, convection), how pressure responds, and how quickly substances transition between states.

Why phases matter in the simulation

The same substance behaves very differently across phases. A solid stores heat differently than a liquid, and a gas responds dramatically to pressure. The wiki groups phase data so students can compare how properties change across states.

Phase transitions

Phase transitions occur when temperature or pressure crosses a threshold for that substance. The simulation uses phase transition data (melting, boiling, critical point) and latent heat to model energy required to change state.
