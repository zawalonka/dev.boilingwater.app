Educational notes

Iridium is second-densest element - incredibly hard and corrosion-resistant! Iridium-platinum crucibles used for extremely high-temperature melting. Iridium spark plug electrodes last longer than platinum. K-T boundary iridium layer marks dinosaur extinction 66 million years ago. Iridium rare on Earth but more common in meteorites. Discovered in 1804. Named from goddess Iris (rainbow) - compounds colorful. Expensive precious metal.
