Educational notes

Protactinium is radioactive actinide - rare and hazardous! Protactinium-231 alpha and beta emitter. Ultra-rare - costs prohibitive. Only used in specialized research. Protactinium-233 produced in thorium reactors. Named because it's parent/protactor of actinium (231→227). Discovered in 1913. Behavior studied via minute quantities. Virtually no practical applications outside research.
