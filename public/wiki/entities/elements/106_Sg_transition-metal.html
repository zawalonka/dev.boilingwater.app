Educational notes

Seaborgium is named after Glenn Seaborg - discoverer of plutonium and many transuranic elements! Seaborgium-271 half-life 3 minutes. Synthesized in 1974. Transactinide element. Chemical identification from decay patterns. Named in 1997 (controversy - Seaborg still living). Predicted similar to tungsten. Named to honor nuclear chemistry pioneer. Research element.
