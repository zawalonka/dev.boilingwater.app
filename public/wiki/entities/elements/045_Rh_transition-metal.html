Educational notes

Rhodium is extremely rare precious metal - more expensive than gold by weight! Catalytic converters in cars use rhodium to reduce pollution. Rhodium-platinum alloys for high-temperature applications (jet engines). Rhodium chloride used in industrial catalysis. Mirror coatings use rhodium (reflects 95%+ light). Discovered in 1803. Named from Greek 'rhodon' meaning rose (of rose-red compounds). Highly resistant to corrosion and heat.
