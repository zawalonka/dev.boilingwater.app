Educational notes

Uranium is radioactive - all uranium atoms spontaneously decay, releasing energy. Uranium-235 undergoes nuclear fission (splits apart) - energy source for nuclear power plants and weapons. Enriching uranium (increasing U-235 from 0.7% to 3-90%) is one of hardest nuclear tasks. Depleted uranium (leftover U-238) is extremely dense - used in armor and armor-piercing ammunition. Marie Curie discovered radioactivity studying uranium. Half-life of U-238 is 4.5 billion years (same age as Earth) - some original uranium remains.
