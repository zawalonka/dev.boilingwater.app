Element · Li

Educational notes

Lightest metal on Earth! Key ingredient in rechargeable lithium-ion batteries powering phones, laptops, and electric cars. Discovered in 1817. Also used in nuclear weapons (lithium-6 produces tritium) and psychiatric medications (lithium carbonate treats bipolar disorder). When heated, burns with brilliant red flame. Reacts vigorously with water. Chile, Argentina, and China are major sources - lithium mining will boom as EV demand increases.
