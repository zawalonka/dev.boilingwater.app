Educational notes

Gallium is a semiconductor like silicon but with different electrical properties - used in solar cells on space probes! Surprising property: melts at 29.8°C (just above room temperature) - you can hold solid gallium in your hand and watch it melt. Discovered in 1875. Gallium arsenide (GaAs) is more efficient at converting light to electricity than silicon. LEDs often use gallium compounds. Extremely pure - must be >99.9999% for electronics.
