Educational notes

Titanium is stronger than steel but 45% lighter - used in aircraft, spacecraft, and prosthetic bones! Medical grade titanium doesn't trigger immune rejection, so it's used in joint replacements and dental implants. Discovered in 1791. Melting point 1,668°C (higher than steel). Titanium dioxide (TiO₂) is white pigment in paint and sunscreen. Military aircraft use titanium extensively - cost is 100× more than steel but weight savings justify it.
