Aluminum

Element · Al

Educational notes

Most abundant metal in Earth's crust! Yet pure aluminum wasn't isolated until 1825 because it bonds so strongly to oxygen. Aluminum foil, cans, and aerospace components are ubiquitous. Aluminum oxide (corundum) forms rubies and sapphires (red and blue crystals). One problem: aluminum recycling takes 95% less energy than mining new ore, but only ~50% of aluminum is recycled. Aluminum reacts with oxygen to form a protective oxide layer - this is why aluminum doesn't rust like iron.
