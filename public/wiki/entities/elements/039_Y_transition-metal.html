Educational notes

Yttrium is rare earth element (despite name, not rare) - critical for modern technology! Yttrium-aluminum-garnet (YAG) lasers cut steel and weld industrial materials. Yttrium superconductors show promise for future power transmission. Yttrium phosphor in color TV screens produces red light. Discovered in 1794 in rare earth minerals. All rare earths named after Ytterby, Sweden. Medical yttrium-90 used to treat liver cancer. High-temperature superconductors use yttrium.
