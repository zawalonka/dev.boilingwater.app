Element · Kr

Educational notes

Krypton is inert noble gas - name means 'hidden' in Greek because so rare! Used in high-efficiency light bulbs (better than argon). Krypton-86 was used to define the meter's length (1960-1983) - showed element's precision. Krypton-85 is radioactive byproduct of nuclear reactors - leaks from weapons plants detected worldwide. Double-pane windows sometimes filled with krypton (more insulating than argon). Discovered in 1898 in air.
