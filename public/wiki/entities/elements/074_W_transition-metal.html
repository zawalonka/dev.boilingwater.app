Element · W

Educational notes

Tungsten has highest melting point of all elements (3,422°C) - used in light bulb filaments! Filament glows white-hot without melting for thousands of hours. Tungsten carbide is extremely hard - drill bits, cutting tools. Tungsten alloys used in military armor-piercing ammunition and weights. Discovered in 1783. Name from Swedish 'tung sten' (heavy stone). Tungsten-186 radioactive (very long half-life). Density high - used as radiation shield.
