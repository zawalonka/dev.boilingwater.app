Educational notes

Chlorine gas (Cl₂) is greenish-yellow and toxic - killed thousands in WWI as a chemical weapon. Despite this, chlorine compounds are essential: table salt (NaCl), bleach (kills bacteria), and pool disinfectant. Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) with chlorine destroyed ozone layer, requiring Montreal Protocol ban. Chlorophyll isn't affected by chlorine despite the name. Chlorine is extracted from salt water by electrolysis - one of the largest industrial processes. Discovered in 1774.
