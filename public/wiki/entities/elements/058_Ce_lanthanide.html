Educational notes

Cerium is most abundant rare earth element - more plentiful than copper! Cerium oxide is polishing powder for fine optical surfaces (removes scratches). Catalytic converters use cerium oxide to reduce emissions. Cerium-140 used in research. Mischmetal (cerium alloy) makes lighter flints. Cerium fluoride used in arc lamps. Oil industry uses cerium as catalyst. Discovered in 1803 (predicted by Mendeleev). Cost varies wildly with rare earth market.
