Educational notes

Rotten eggs smell like hydrogen sulfide - one of sulfur's compounds! Sulfur burns with blue flame producing sulfur dioxide (SO₂). Discovered in ancient times; sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄) is the most-produced chemical globally - used in batteries, fertilizers, and metal processing. Vulcanization (heating rubber with sulfur) makes it strong and elastic - invented by Charles Goodyear. Some amino acids need sulfur. Sulfur deposits around hot springs and volcanoes form yellow crystals.
