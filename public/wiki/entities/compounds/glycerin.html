Glycerin

Compound

Educational notes

Boiling point 290°C - extremely high! This is because glycerin has THREE hydroxyl (OH) groups, allowing extensive hydrogen bonding. Very viscous, thick, syrupy liquid. Freezes at 17.8°C - can solidify in cold weather. Used in antifreeze, cosmetics (holds moisture), and soap.
